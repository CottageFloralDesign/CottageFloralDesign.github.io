Meet the Designer

The Story-Teller
Behind the Blooms

Owner & Lead Floral Designer

What began as a love for knowing the names of wildflowers has grown into something more intentional. I founded Cottage Floral Design with the belief that fresh florals should do more than decorate a space. They should draw people in, mark a moment, and tell a story.

Flowers are a luxury product and deeply perishable. To me, that is exactly what makes them meaningful. Their fleeting nature invites us to be present. When you bring flowers into a moment you want to celebrate, they ask you to slow down, to notice, and to feel it more fully.

I also believe flowers exist for a reason beyond function. There are countless ways plants reproduce, and yet we are given something this intricate and beautiful. To me, that reflects a Creator who values beauty, and reminds me that beauty itself is a worthy pursuit.

With a background in plant science and formal floral training, I approach each piece with both creativity and care, designing florals that not only look beautiful but move well and last throughout your day.

I have always been drawn to people and their stories, and that curiosity shapes everything I create. Inspired by the Dutch masters and the layered symbolism of florals throughout history, I design with intention, creating pieces that feel personal, expressive, and alive.

Our Philosophy

What We Believe

❀

Story First

Every design begins with meaning. Flowers become a way to hold memory, emotion, and story in a physical, fleeting form.

✦

Presence Over Perfection

Because flowers are fleeting, they ask us to be present. Their impermanence is not a flaw, but the very reason they matter.

✿

Floral Design as Art

We approach florals as a study of form, movement, and space, creating pieces that feel expressive, sculptural, and alive.

❁

Grounded in Botany

A foundation in plant science and formal training informs each design, honoring how flowers naturally grow, move, and exist.

✧

Thoughtful Stewardship

Whenever possible, we work with seasonal materials and sustainable mechanics, designing with both beauty and responsibility in mind.

❋

Rooted in Meaning

This work is shaped by faith and a belief that beauty has purpose, and that creating it is a meaningful pursuit.
